Lewis Hamilton is a British Formula 1 driver widely regarded as one of the greatest in the sport’s history.

Born on January 7, 1985, in Stevenage, England, Hamilton began karting at a young age and quickly rose through the ranks.

He made his F1 debut with McLaren in 2007, nearly winning the championship in his rookie season. In 2008, he became the youngest world champion at the time.

Hamilton later joined Mercedes in 2013, where he dominated the sport, winning six more world championships (2014, 2015, 2017, 2018, 2019, 2020).

Known for his aggressive driving style, unmatched consistency, and qualifying speed, he holds numerous records, including most wins, pole positions, and podium finishes.

Beyond the track, Hamilton is an advocate for diversity, animal rights, and sustainability.

He launched the Mission 44 foundation to support underrepresented youth and is a vocal supporter of the Black Lives Matter movement.

Hamilton is also involved in fashion and music, showcasing his wide-ranging talents.

His legacy in F1 extends beyond statistics—he is a transformative figure in both sport and society.
